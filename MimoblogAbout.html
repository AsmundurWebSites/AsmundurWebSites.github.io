Chef

In a number of restaurants in Iceland and one in Norway from canteens to fine dining restaurants.

The roles I had were as many as the restaurants

I have been everything from the kitchen porter to a head chef.

These postions involved:

  • Manageing shifts: Deciding about who is in on what day, and how shifts are structured, calling extras, hire/fire people
  • In the kitchen there also needs to be a cleaning plan, safety plan and a training plan(for new employes)!
  • Making sure the cleaning plan was being followed to the end of the day, then lists were checked.
  • Then the cleaning plan had a day, week, and a month specific items to do.On some days whole cooler cleaned, some days ventilation system.
  • Managing inventory: making sure there is enough of produce to cook everything on the menu. Most times plan some days/week ahead.
  • Managing inventory also included prices checking and some times price chasing.
  • Call repairmen if equipment broke. And on rare ocastions maintanence on the building painting for example.
  • Quite a few times I was doing all the above mentioned while also being an active member on the shift, prepairing for the menu
  • Prepairing for the menu required knowledge of vegetables,fruits,meats,breads,dairy,cross contamination,cleaning, basic first aid, being organized and many more.
  • It also calls for planing of the the day: Decision about what to do that day, go over inventory and make decisions about freshness,quility,quantity
  • It also icludes a working schedule at least for you, because some of the things take all day while others take 10 minutes.
  • Knowing how to cook everything for each menu: Cooking methods, Cooking time, cutting.
  • Then there has to be an order on what you do according to the cleaning around you and your equipment.
  • And cleaning meant a small cleaning session where you clean the nearest workspace also if you could, and arrange produce, equipment.
